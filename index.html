1 The 50/50 Parity Objective

The fundamental goal of this partnership is to maintain an equal split in ownership, responsibility, and equity. Both parties agree that any deviation from equal payments is a temporary imbalance that must be corrected to maintain the integrity of the agreement.

  • Monthly Obligation: Mortgage, taxes, and insurance are split exactly 50/50.
  • Catch-Up Goal: If one party covers a gap, the primary financial priority of the delinquent party is to reimburse the payer as soon as funds are available.

2 Payment Imbalance & Equity Logic

In the event that the "Catch-Up" goal is not met prior to a sale or refinancing, the following Debt-First Model shall be applied:

"Any over-contribution by one party is recorded as a 'Contribution Credit.' Before the final 50/50 split of net proceeds, the party with the credit is paid back in full from the top of the profit."

3 Operating & Maintenance

Routine Costs

Minor repairs under $500 are handled as they arise and balanced monthly to keep the 50/50 ratio.

Major Improvements

Expenses over $500 require mutual written consent before the work commences.

4 Exit Strategy & Right of First Refusal

If one owner wishes to sell their interest, they must offer it to the other owner first at fair market value. If a buy-out is not possible, the house will be listed for sale to the public.
